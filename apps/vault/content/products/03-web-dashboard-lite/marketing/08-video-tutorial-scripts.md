- 15:00-17:00: Rp 200K (slow hours)
- 18:00-20:00: Rp 1.5M (dinner rush)  -> **Peak!**
- 21:00-23:00: Rp 300K (closing)

Peak hours clearly: 12-2 PM, 6-8 PM."

[CURRENT STAFFING PROBLEMS - 1:00-2:00]
[Show staffing issues]

"Masalah typical:

**Problem 1: Understaffing at Peak**
[Show 12-2 PM data]
- Sales: Rp 2M (highest)
- Current staff: 2 orang
- Result: Long wait times (10-15 minutes), customer complaints

**Problem 2: Overstaffing at Slow Hours**
[Show 3-5 PM data]
- Sales: Rp 200K (lowest)
- Current staff: 2 orang (same as peak!)
- Result: Staff idle, wasted labor cost

**Problem 3: Flat Staffing Schedule**
Many businesses use same staffing all day:
- 10 AM - 10 PM: 2 staff (no variation)
- This ignores demand patterns!"

[OPTIMIZED STAFFING - 2:00-3:30]
[Show optimization plan]

"Cara optimize:

**Step 1: Calculate Staff Needed per Hour**
Formula: Staff needed = Sales per hour / Rp 1M (rule of thumb: 1 staff handles Rp 1M sales/hour)

Contoh:
- 12-2 PM: Rp 2M  ->  2 staff
- 3-5 PM: Rp 200K  ->  0.2 staff (round up to 1)
- 6-8 PM: Rp 1.5M  ->  1.5 staff (round up to 2)

**Step 2: Create Shift Schedule**

Old schedule (flat):
- Shift 1: 10 AM - 6 PM (2 staff)
- Shift 2: 6 PM - 10 PM (2 staff)
- Total: 2 staff Ã— 12 hours = 24 staff-hours/day

New schedule (optimized):
- Shift 1 (Breakfast): 7-11 AM (1 staff)
- Shift 2 (Lunch Rush): 11 AM - 3 PM (3 staff)  -> **Add 1 staff at peak**
- Shift 3 (Afternoon Slow): 3-6 PM (1 staff)  -> **Reduce to 1**
- Shift 4 (Dinner Rush): 6-9 PM (2 staff)
- Shift 5 (Closing): 9-11 PM (1 staff)
- Total: 20 staff-hours/day

**Savings: 4 staff-hours/day = 120 hours/month = Rp 600K saved (at Rp 5K/hour)!**"

- Change: +Rp 2M (+8.7%)  -> [x] Healthy growth"

[INTERPRETATION - 0:45-2:00]
[Explain what each metric means]

"Cara interpret:

**Day-over-Day (DoD):**
- **Use:** Short-term fluctuations (marketing campaign, weather, events)
- **Example:** If DoD +20%, check: 'Apakah ada event? Promo? Ramai karena apa?'
- **Caution:** DoD volatile (weekday vs weekend, holiday vs normal day)

**Week-over-Week (WoW):**
- **Use:** Medium-term trends (1-month patterns)
- **Example:** WoW +10% for 4 weeks straight  ->  Clear growth trend
- **Best for:** Tracking marketing campaign impact (1-month campaign)

**Month-over-Month (MoM):**
- **Use:** Long-term business health (annual growth rate)
- **Example:** MoM +8% sustained = 96% annual growth (doubling every year!)
- **Best for:** Investor reporting, strategic decisions

**Benchmark:**
- DoD: ±5% (normal daily variance)
- WoW: +5-10% (good growth)
- MoM: +5-15% (healthy SME growth)"

[RED FLAGS - 2:00-3:00]
[Show negative growth scenarios]

"Red flags to watch:

**Scenario 1: Consistent Decline**
- WoW: -5%, -8%, -12% (3 weeks in a row)
- **Action:** Investigate ASAP! Check:
 - New competitor nearby?
 - Quality issues (customer complaints)?
 - Price too high?

**Scenario 2: Plateau**
- MoM: +0%, +1%, +0% (3 months flat)
- **Action:** Business stagnant. Need:
 - New menu items (innovation)
 - Marketing campaign (attract new customers)
 - Price optimization (increase revenue)

**Scenario 3: Erratic Growth**
- DoD: +20%, -15%, +10%, -8% (wild swings)
- **Cause:** External factors (weekend vs weekday, weather)
- **Action:** Focus on WoW/MoM (smoother trends), ignore DoD volatility"

[CASE STUDY - 3:00-3:30]
[Show example]

**Minimum Setup:**
- Microphone: USB condenser mic (Rp 500K-1M) - better than laptop mic

## 8. Performance Metrics

### 8.1 YouTube Analytics to Track

**Engagement Metrics:**
- Watch Time (goal: >50% avg view duration)
- Click-Through Rate (CTR) on thumbnails (goal: >5%)
- Likes / Dislikes ratio (goal: >95% likes)
- Comments (goal: >10 comments/video for engagement)

**Conversion Metrics:**
- Link clicks in description (e1-dashboard.com)
- Free tier signups attributed to YouTube (UTM: ?utm_source=youtube)
- Paid conversions from YouTube users (track in analytics)

**Growth Metrics:**
- Subscriber growth rate (goal: +50 subs/week)
- Video views (goal: 500+ views/video in first month)

### 8.2 A/B Testing

**Test 1: Thumbnail Style**
- Version A: Screenshot-heavy (70% screenshot, 30% text)
- Version B: Text-heavy (70% text, 30% screenshot)
- Metric: CTR

**Test 2: Video Length**
- Version A: Short (3-4 minutes)
- Version B: Long (6-8 minutes)
- Metric: Watch time & completion rate

**Test 3: Language**
- Version A: 100% Bahasa Indonesia
- Version B: Bilingual (Bahasa + English subtitles)
- Metric: International views (outside Indonesia)
