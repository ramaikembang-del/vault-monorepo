## 5. Break-Even Analysis

### 5.1 How Many Support Incidents Justify Subscription?

**Annual subscription cost:** Rp 750,000
**Support incident cost:** Rp 500,000 per incident

**Break-even calculation:**
```
Break-even incidents = Annual Subscription Cost / Support Incident Cost
 = Rp 750,000 / Rp 500,000
 = 1.5 incidents/year
```

**Conclusion:** If you need support **2+ times per year**, the subscription pays for itself (ignoring updates, bug fixes, etc.).

---

### 5.2 How Much Is Template Rebuild Worth?

**Template rebuild cost:** Rp 2,000,000 (every 2 years)
**2-year subscription cost:** Rp 1,500,000

**Calculation:**
```
Value of avoiding rebuild = Rp 2,000,000
Subscription cost = Rp 1,500,000
Net savings = Rp 500,000
```

**Conclusion:** Even if you NEVER use support, the subscription saves **Rp 500K** over 2 years by avoiding template rebuild. This alone justifies the cost.

---

### 5.3 What's Your Time Worth?

**Scenario:** You troubleshoot a data refresh error for 2 hours.

**Opportunity cost (owner/manager time):**
```
Time spent troubleshooting: 2 hours
Opportunity cost: Rp 500,000/hour (what you could earn doing business development, etc.)
Total opportunity cost: 2 hours × Rp 500,000 = Rp 1,000,000
```

**With subscription:**
- Send WhatsApp message (2 minutes)
- Receive solution within 24 hours
- Total time spent: 5 minutes (reading response, applying fix)
- Opportunity cost: Rp 500,000/hour × (5/60) = Rp 42,000

**Savings:** Rp 1,000,000 - Rp 42,000 = **Rp 958,000 saved per incident**

**Conclusion:** A single support incident saves **Rp 1M** in opportunity cost. Subscription pays for itself after **1 incident** (or <1 month for typical users).

## 10. Conclusion & Recommendations

### 10.1 For Sales Team

**When to push subscription hard (High ROI scenarios):**
- Customer uses dashboard weekly/monthly (high engagement)
- Customer has limited technical skills (will need support)
- Customer values time (high opportunity cost, Rp 500K+/hour)
- Customer relies on analytics for critical decisions (downtime = lost revenue)

**When to be honest (Low ROI scenarios):**
- Customer uses dashboard quarterly (infrequent, may not need monthly updates)
- Customer has in-house data analyst (can DIY troubleshooting)
- Customer is price-sensitive and budget-constrained (Rp 75K/month is significant)

**Recommendation:** Be transparent. If customer truly doesn't need subscription, tell them. They can always subscribe later. Building trust → long-term customer relationship.

### 10.2 For Prospects

**You should subscribe if:**
1. You use the template at least monthly
2. You value your time at Rp 200K+/hour
3. You want peace of mind (no surprises when POS updates)
