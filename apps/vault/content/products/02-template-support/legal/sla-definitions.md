# Template Support Service - Service Level Agreement (SLA)

**Product:** R1 - Template Support & Subscription
**Effective Date:** January 6, 2025
**Version:** 1.0
**SLA Period:** Monthly (reviewed quarterly)

---

## 1. SLA Overview

This Service Level Agreement (SLA) defines the service commitments for Template Support subscribers. It supplements the Subscription Terms & Conditions.

**Covered Services:**
- Template updates and releases
- Priority email and WhatsApp support
- Early access to beta features
- Learning resources and tutorials

**Not Covered by SLA:**
- Custom development or consulting (requires separate agreement)
- Issues caused by user modifications to template
- Third-party software issues (POS systems, Power BI Desktop, Microsoft)
- Force majeure events (Section 6)

### 2.2 Standard Support (Non-Subscribers)

| Channel | Weekdays (Mon-Fri) | Weekends (Sat) | Sundays & Holidays |
|---------|-------------------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Email** | <48 hours | <72 hours | <96 hours |
| **WhatsApp** | Not available | Not available | Not available |

**Upgrade Benefit:** Subscribers get 2x faster response time + WhatsApp access.

### 2.3 Response Time Measurement

**"Response Time"** = Time from ticket submission to our first substantive reply

**First Response Must Include:**
- Acknowledgment of the issue
- Initial assessment or troubleshooting steps
- Estimated time to resolution (if known)
- Request for additional information (if needed)

**Response Time STARTS:**
- When ticket is received during business hours
- At start of next business day if received outside business hours

**Response Time EXCLUDES:**
- Weekends and Indonesian public holidays (unless emergency)
- Time waiting for customer to provide requested information
- Time for customer to test proposed solutions

### 2.4 Resolution Time

**Resolution is NOT guaranteed within a specific timeframe.**

We commit to:
- Best effort resolution as quickly as possible
- Regular status updates (every 48 hours for open tickets)
- Escalation to senior support if issue persists >5 business days

**Complex Issues:** Some issues may require template updates or code changes, which follow the Update Release Schedule (Section 3).

## 3. Template Update Release Schedule

### 3.1 Update Frequency

**Minimum Commitment:** 1 template update per quarter (every 3 months)

**Target Frequency:** 1 update per month (aspirational, not guaranteed)

**Actual Cadence:** Based on:
- User-reported bugs (critical bugs released ASAP)
- Feature requests (prioritized by subscriber votes)
- POS compatibility needs (new POS formats)
- Power BI version updates (Microsoft releases)

### 3.2 Update Types

**Critical Updates (Released ASAP):**
- **Security vulnerabilities** - Within 48 hours of discovery
- **Data-breaking bugs** - Within 7 days (prevents dashboard from functioning)
- **POS compatibility breaks** - Within 14 days (major POS system changes)

**Standard Updates (Monthly Cadence):**
- New metrics or dashboard pages
- Visual enhancements (better charts, themes)
- Performance improvements (faster refresh times)
- Bug fixes (non-critical issues)

**Major Updates (Quarterly):**
- Significant feature additions (new analysis modules)
- Redesigns or major UX improvements
- New POS system integrations

### 3.3 Update Delivery

**Delivery Method:**
- Email notification with download link
- Release notes detailing changes
- Migration guide (if needed)

**Delivery Timeframe:**
- Email sent within 24 hours of update release
- Download link valid for 90 days
- Previous template versions remain functional (no forced updates)

**Testing:** All updates tested on minimum 2 test datasets before release.

## 5. Service Levels by Severity

### 5.1 Severity Classifications

**Critical (Sev 1):**
- Template completely broken (won't open or refresh)
- Data loss or corruption
- Security vulnerability discovered

**Response:** <4 hours (during business hours), <12 hours (outside business hours)
**Escalation:** Immediate escalation to senior support + founder notification

---

**High (Sev 2):**
- Major feature not working (entire dashboard tab broken)
- Incorrect calculations affecting business decisions
- POS data import fails for all data sources

**Response:** <24 hours (weekdays), <48 hours (weekends)
**Escalation:** After 48 hours if not resolved

---

**Medium (Sev 3):**
- Single metric or visual not working
- Minor calculation errors (affects 1-2 metrics)
- Slow performance (but still functional)

**Response:** <24 hours (weekdays), <72 hours (weekends)
**Escalation:** After 5 business days if not resolved

---

**Low (Sev 4):**
- Cosmetic issues (colors, fonts, alignment)
- Feature requests or enhancements
- General questions about usage

**Response:** <48 hours (weekdays), <96 hours (weekends)
**Escalation:** Not escalated (resolved in normal queue)

### 5.2 Severity Assessment

**Who Determines Severity:**
- Initial assessment by support team (based on description)
- May be adjusted after investigation
- Customer can request severity review if they disagree

**Severity Escalation:**
- Customer can request higher severity if business impact is critical
- Support lead reviews and approves/denies escalation within 2 hours
- Denial explained with rationale

## 6. Exclusions & Limitations

### 6.1 SLA Does NOT Cover

**Issues Caused By:**
- User modifications to template structure
- Use of outdated template version (>2 versions behind)
- Incompatible Power BI Desktop version (must use recommended version)
- Corrupted or malformed data files
- Third-party software failures (POS systems, Excel, databases)
- Hardware or network issues on customer side
- Power BI Desktop bugs (report to Microsoft, not us)

**Services Outside Scope:**
- Custom template modifications (requires consulting agreement)
- Business consulting unrelated to template usage
- Data cleanup or transformation services
- Training beyond self-service resources

### 6.2 Force Majeure

SLA commitments are suspended during force majeure events:
- Natural disasters (earthquakes, floods, fires)
- Government actions, war, terrorism, civil unrest
- Internet or telecommunications outages
- Third-party service provider failures (Google Drive, email)
- Pandemic, epidemic, or public health emergency

**Notice:** We will notify subscribers of force majeure events within 24 hours and provide status updates every 48 hours.

## 7. Service Credits (Remedies for SLA Breach)

### 7.1 Service Credit Eligibility

You are eligible for service credits if:
- You are an active paid subscriber
- We miss response time commitment by >50% (e.g., 36+ hours when <24h promised)
- You request credit within 30 days of SLA breach
- Issue was not caused by exclusions (Section 6.1)

### 7.2 Service Credit Amounts

| SLA Breach | Service Credit |
|------------|----------------|
| **Response Time:** 2x late (e.g., 48h when <24h promised) | 10% of monthly fee (Rp 7,500) |
| **Response Time:** 3x+ late (e.g., 72h+ when <24h promised) | 25% of monthly fee (Rp 18,750) |
| **No Response:** >7 days without response | 50% of monthly fee (Rp 37,500) |
| **Update Cadence:** 0 updates in 6 months | 1 month free (Rp 75,000) |

**Maximum Credits:** 50% of annual subscription fee (Rp 375,000 for annual, Rp 450,000 for 12 monthly payments)

### 7.3 Claiming Service Credits

To claim service credit:
1. Email sla-claim@[company].com within 30 days of breach
2. Include ticket number and timestamp of request
3. Describe the SLA breach (e.g., "Response took 50 hours, SLA is 24 hours")
4. We will investigate and respond within 7 business days

**Credit Application:**
- Applied to next billing cycle (not cash refund)
- If you cancel before credit is applied, credit is forfeited
- Cannot be combined with other discounts or promotions

### 7.4 Service Credits Are Sole Remedy

**Service credits are your ONLY remedy for SLA breaches.**

You waive the right to:
- Monetary damages beyond service credits
- Cancellation without notice due to SLA breach (must follow standard cancellation process)
- Withholding payment for SLA breaches

See Subscription Terms Section 8 (Limitation of Liability) for details.

## 9. Escalation Path

### 9.1 Support Escalation Process

**Level 1: Initial Support (First Response)**
- **Who:** Support Associate
- **Handles:** Routine questions, common issues, basic troubleshooting
- **Response Time:** Per SLA (Section 2)

**Level 2: Senior Support (Escalated After 48 Hours)**
- **Who:** Senior Support Engineer
- **Handles:** Complex technical issues, template bugs, advanced troubleshooting
- **Escalation Trigger:** Ticket open >48 hours OR customer requests escalation

**Level 3: Product Lead (Escalated After 5 Days)**
- **Who:** Product Lead / Founder
- **Handles:** Template modifications needed, feature requests, systemic issues
- **Escalation Trigger:** Ticket open >5 business days OR critical severity

### 9.2 Customer-Initiated Escalation

You may request escalation at any time by:
- Replying to ticket with "ESCALATE" in subject line
- Calling [Phone Number] during business hours
- WhatsApp message to [Number] with "Urgent Escalation: [Ticket #]"

**Escalation Review:** Senior support reviews within 2 hours and responds with:
- Acknowledgment of escalation
- Revised timeline for resolution
- Escalation justification or denial (if not warranted)
