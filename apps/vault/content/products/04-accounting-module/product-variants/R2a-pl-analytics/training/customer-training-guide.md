# R2a: Simple P&L - Training Guide

**Last Updated:** 2025-12-27

---

## Quick Start

### What is R2a?
Simple Profit & Loss statement for F&B businesses. See if you're making money!

### First Setup (10 min)
1. Connect revenue data (from POS or manual)
2. Enter your expense categories (rent, utilities, salaries, etc.)
3. Enter monthly expenses
4. View instant P&L!

---

## Key Concepts

**Revenue:** All sales (from POS)
**COGS:** Cost of Goods Sold (food/beverage costs)
**Operating Expenses:** Rent, utilities, salaries, marketing, etc.
**Net Profit:** Revenue - COGS - Expenses

**Food Cost %:** COGS / Revenue (target: 25-35% for restaurants)
**Profit Margin:** Net Profit / Revenue (target: 10-15% healthy)

---

## Entering Expenses

**One-time Setup:**
1. Settings → Expense Categories
2. Add your categories:
 - Rent
 - Utilities (electric, water, gas)
 - Salaries & wages
 - Marketing
 - Repairs & maintenance
 - Delivery/logistics
 - Licenses & permits
 - Bank fees
 - Miscellaneous

**Monthly Entry:**
1. P&L → Enter Expenses
2. For each category, enter amount
3. Add notes if needed
4. Save

**Tip:** Do this on 1st of month for previous month

---

## Reading Your P&L

**Sample P&L:**
```
Revenue: Rp 100,000,000
COGS (30%): Rp 30,000,000
Gross Profit: Rp 70,000,000

Operating Expenses:
 Rent: Rp 15,000,000
 Salaries: Rp 25,000,000
 Utilities: Rp 5,000,000
 Other: Rp 10,000,000
Total Expenses: Rp 55,000,000

Net Profit: Rp 15,000,000 (15% margin)
```

**Red flags:**
- Negative profit (losing money)
- Food cost >40%
- Profit margin <5%

## Advanced Features

**Budget vs Actual:**
1. Set monthly budget per category
2. R2a shows variance
3. Identify overspending

**Multi-Outlet:**
- View each outlet separately
- Consolidated company P&L
- Compare performance

**Export:**
- PDF for accountant
- Excel for analysis
- Accurate Online / Jurnal integration

---

## Common Questions

**"Do I need an accountant?"**
- R2a simplifies, but accountant still helpful for taxes
- Use R2a for daily decisions, accountant for year-end

**"What about depreciation, amortization?"**
- R2a is simplified
- For full accounting, use Accurate/Jurnal
- R2a focuses on cash-based decisions
