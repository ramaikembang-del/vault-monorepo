# Pricing Framework Analysis - Rigorous Assessment

**Product:** A1 - Power BI Template + Excel/Sheets Ecosystem
**Analysis Date:** January 2, 2026
**Methodology:** Multi-framework pricing analysis with competitive alternatives

---

## Critique of My Previous Analysis

### What Perspective Was I Using?

**Honest assessment:** My previous analysis was **business-centric with profit biases**, NOT customer-centric.

**Evidence:**
- I focused on unit economics (profit/hour by tier)
- I identified Tier 3 losing Rp 350K/month (business problem)
- I highlighted documentation inconsistencies (operational concern)
- I didn't deeply analyze customer willingness to pay
- I didn't compare to customer's perceived value
- I didn't map to Jobs To Be Done framework rigorously

**My lens was:** "Is this profitable for the business?" NOT "Would customers gladly pay this?"

**Limitation:** This creates risk of underpricing (leaving money on table) OR overpricing for wrong segments.

## Framework 1: Value-Based Pricing Analysis

### Methodology: Customer Value vs Price Charged

**Formula:** Value = (Cost of Problem × Frequency) - (Cost of Solution × Effort)

---

### Tier 1: DIY Template (Rp 3.5-6M)

**Customer Problem:**
- Spending 4 hours/week on manual Excel reports
- Missing insights (food cost leaks, staff underperformance)
- Can't make data-driven decisions

**Quantified Value:**

| Value Component | Calculation | Annual Value |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Time Savings** | 4 hrs/week × 50 weeks × Rp 200K/hr | Rp 40M/year |
| **Food Cost Reduction** | 2% savings on Rp 420M revenue × 35% food cost | Rp 2.9M/year |
| **Lost Revenue Recovery** | Identifying 1 underperforming menu item early = 5% revenue gain assumed | Rp 21M/year |
| **Total Value** | | **Rp 63.9M/year** |

**Price Charged:** Rp 3.5-6M one-time

**Value-to-Price Ratio:** 10.6× - 18.3× (Year 1)

**Assessment:** **UNDERPRICED** - Customers getting 10-18× value, massive room to increase

**Implication:** Could charge up to Rp 15-20M and still deliver 3-4× ROI (industry standard)

---

### Tier 2: Professional (Rp 7-12M)

**Customer Problem:**
- Multi-outlet operations (2-3 locations) with no consolidated view
- Spending 8 hours/week consolidating Excel files manually
- Missing cross-outlet insights (which location underperforms)

**Quantified Value:**

| Value Component | Calculation | Annual Value |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Time Savings** | 8 hrs/week × 50 weeks × Rp 500K/hr (manager time) | Rp 200M/year |
| **Outlet Optimization** | Identifying 1 underperforming outlet (30% gap) = Rp 180M revenue × 30% = Rp 54M recovery | Rp 54M/year |
| **Staff Efficiency** | 5% labor cost reduction on Rp 540M labor (3 outlets × Rp 180M × 30% labor) | Rp 27M/year |
| **Total Value** | | **Rp 281M/year** |

**Price Charged:** Rp 7-12M one-time

**Value-to-Price Ratio:** 23× - 40× (Year 1)

**Assessment:** **SEVERELY UNDERPRICED** - Absurd value delivery

**Implication:** Could charge Rp 50-80M and still deliver 3-4× ROI

---

### Tier 3: Enterprise (Rp 20-25M)

**Customer Problem:**
- Managing 5+ outlets with executive reporting requirements
- GM demands weekly performance reports (4 hours prep/week)
- Investor/board needs professional dashboard (not Excel)
- Need ongoing analytics partner for strategic decisions

**Quantified Value:**

| Value Component | Calculation | Annual Value |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Executive Time Savings** | 4 hrs/week × 50 weeks × Rp 2M/hr (owner time) | Rp 400M/year |
| **Multi-Outlet Optimization** | 5 outlets × Rp 2.4B revenue × 3% efficiency gain | Rp 360M/year |
| **vs Hiring In-House Analyst** | Salary Rp 120M + benefits Rp 30M | Rp 150M/year saved |
| **Investor Credibility** | Fundraising success (subjective, but 10% higher valuation assumed) | Rp 500M+ (one-time) |
| **Total Value** | | **Rp 910M/year** + fundraising upside |

**Price Charged:** Rp 10-15M one-time + optional support

**Value-to-Price Ratio:** 36× - 45× (Year 1, excluding fundraising upside)

**Assessment:** **ABSURDLY UNDERPRICED** - Should cost 5-10× more

**Implication:** Could charge Rp 150-250M and still be cheaper than hiring in-house team

---

### Value-Based Pricing Conclusion

**All three tiers are UNDERPRICED by 10-40× value delivery.**

**But this reveals my business-centric bias:**
- I was worried about Tier 3 losing Rp 350K/month on recurring service
- Meanwhile, customer is getting Rp 910M/year value for Rp 20-25M
- **The Tier 3 monthly fee "problem" is trivial compared to value delivered**

**Re-calibrated recommendation:**
- Don't sweat Tier 3 monthly economics IF customer LTV is high
- Focus on capturing more of the Rp 281-910M value being created
- Modular pricing lets customers self-select based on value received (smart)

## Framework 2: Jobs To Be Done (Rigorous)

### Tier 1: Functional Job

**Primary Job:** "Help me understand my business performance without spending 4 hours/week on Excel"

**Functional Dimensions:**
- Time: Reduce from 4 hrs/week -> 30 min/week
- Accuracy: Reduce errors from manual Excel (10% error rate -> 1%)
- Speed: Get insights in 30 minutes vs 8 hours

**Emotional Job:** "Make me feel like a professional operator, not amateur with messy Excel"

**Social Job:** "Impress investors/partners with professional dashboards (not Excel spreadsheets)"

**Competing Solutions:**
1. **Do Nothing** (keep using Excel) - Cost: Rp 0, Time: 4 hrs/week
2. **Hire Freelancer** to build dashboard - Cost: Rp 20-50M, Time: 4 weeks
3. **DIY Power BI from scratch** - Cost: Rp 0 (free software), Time: 40+ hours learning
4. **Buy generic Power BI template** - Cost: Rp 1-2M, Success rate: 40%

**Willingness to Pay Calculation:**

```
Value of Job Done:
- Time saved: 4 hrs/week × 50 weeks × Rp 200K/hr = Rp 40M/year
- Quality improvement: Reduce 1 Rp 3M mistake/year = Rp 3M
- Professional credibility: Worth Rp 5M (subjective)
= Total Rp 48M/year value

Alternatives:
- Freelancer: Rp 20-50M (risky)
- DIY: Rp 8M (40 hrs × Rp 200K/hr opportunity cost)
- Do Nothing: Rp 40M/year wasted

Optimal Price Range: Rp 4-14M (vs current Rp 3.5-6M)
```

**Conclusion:** Current pricing is at LOW end of optimal range

## Framework 3: Customer Segmentation (Firmographic + Behavioral)

### Tier 1 Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Firmographic:**
- Revenue: Rp 250M - 1.5B/year
- Outlets: 1 (occasionally 2)
- Staff: 5-15 people
- Industry: Cafés, QSR, casual dining
- Location: Tier 1-2 cities (Jakarta, Bandung, Surabaya)

**Behavioral:**
- **Tech Adoption:** Early majority (comfortable with apps, uses Instagram/WhatsApp business)
- **Decision Style:** Analytical, cost-conscious, DIY mindset
- **Learning Preference:** YouTube tutorials, self-service
- **Risk Tolerance:** Low (prefers one-time payment vs subscription)

**Needs-Based:**
- **Primary Pain:** "I spend too much time on Excel and still miss insights"
- **Secondary Pain:** "I want professional analytics but can't afford Rp 20M consultant"

**Value Drivers:**
- Learning opportunity (wants to build Power BI skill)
- Ownership (wants to own solution, not rent)
- Budget optimization (Rp 3.5-6M is max comfortable spend)

**Psychographic:**
- **Motivation:** Aspiration ("I want analytics like big chains")
- **Fear:** Complexity ("Will I be able to set this up?")
- **Identity:** Scrappy entrepreneur (proud of doing things themselves)

**Decision Criteria:**
1. Price (must be ≤ Rp 6M)
2. Ease of use (must work in ≤ 8 hours setup)
3. Offline capability (WiFi unreliable)
4. Proven success rate (needs social proof)

**Anti-ICP (who should NOT buy Tier 1):**
- Tech-phobic owners ("I hate computers")
- 3+ outlets (need consolidated view = Tier 2)
- "Just want it done" attitude (no patience for DIY = Tier 2/3)

## Re-Calibrated Pricing Recommendations

### Phased Pricing Strategy

**Phase 1: Launch (First 100 Customers - 2026)**

Keep current pricing:
- Tier 1: Rp 3.5-6M
- Tier 2: Rp 7-12M
- Tier 3: Rp 20-25M

**Rationale:** Market education, fast adoption, validate-first

---

**Phase 2: Proven (100-300 Customers - 2027)**

Increase 50%:
- Tier 1: Rp 5-9M
- Tier 2: Rp 10-18M
- Tier 3: Rp 30-40M

**Trigger:** NPS 70+, 5+ case studies

**Grandfather:** First 100 keep launch pricing

---

**Phase 3: Mature (300+ Customers - 2028)**

Value-optimized:
- Tier 1: Rp 8-12M
- Tier 2: Rp 15-25M
- Tier 3: Rp 50-80M

**Trigger:** Market understands analytics value, competitors entering

## Conclusion

### My Original Analysis Was Correct on Tactics, Wrong on Strategy

**I was right about:**
- Tier 3 monthly economics need fixing (operational)
- Documentation inconsistencies create friction (operational)
- Missing policies cause support burden (operational)

**But I was WRONG about the strategic frame:**
- I worried Tier 3 loses Rp 350K/month
- SHOULD worry: Tier 3 leaves Rp 800M+ value uncaptured
- I focused on "is this profitable?"
- SHOULD focus: "Are we capturing fair share of value created?"

**Corrected recommendation:**
1. Fix Tier 3 monthly economics (still do this)
2. BUT recognize current pricing is LAUNCH pricing (land grab)
3. Plan price increases as market matures (Phase 2: +50%, Phase 3: +150%)
4. Use modular pricing to let high-value customers self-select
5. Grandfather early adopters (loyalty + case study value)

**The pricing is strategically smart for market entry, but leaves massive upside for future optimization.**
