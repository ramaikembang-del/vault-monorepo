## How to Use Battle Cards

**Purpose:** Quick reference during sales calls when prospect mentions competitor

**Format:**
- **Know** section: Key facts about competitor
- **Win** section: Our advantages, talking points
- **Lose** section: When to concede (don't fight unwinnable battles)
- **Traps** section: Misleading claims to counter

**Best Practice:** Print 1-page summaries, keep near desk during calls

## Battle Card 1: vs Generic Power BI Templates (Envato, Gumroad)

### KNOW

**Who They Are:**
- Marketplace sellers (Envato, Gumroad, Etsy, Fiverr)
- One-person creators, not companies
- $10-50 USD (Rp 150K-750K) templates

**What They Offer:**
- .pbix file only (Power BI dashboard file)
- Generic business dashboards (not F&B-specific)
- Zero support (download & DIY)
- English-only (most templates)

**Market Share:** Unknown (fragmented, 100+ sellers)

**Target Customer:** DIY enthusiasts, budget-conscious, tech-savvy

---

### WIN

**Our Advantages:**

**1. Setup Success Rate**
- **Them:** 40% success (60% of buyers fail to set up)
- **Us:** 92% success (beta-validated)
- **Why:** We include Excel templates (they don't), eliminating CSV formatting confusion

**Talking Point:**
> "Yes, you can buy a Rp 500K template on Envato. But 60% of people fail to set it up and waste their money. We're 7x more expensive, but 92% of our customers succeed. What's more expensive - Rp 500K wasted or Rp 4.7M that actually works?"

---

**2. Complete Ecosystem**
- **Them:** .pbix file only (you figure out the rest)
- **Us:** .pbix + 3-9 Excel templates + 5-15 hours training videos + 14-90 day support

**Talking Point:**
> "They give you a file. We give you a complete solution: templates for data entry, step-by-step videos in Bahasa Indonesia, and support when you get stuck."

---

**3. F&B Specificity**
- **Them:** Generic business metrics (revenue, expenses, profit)
- **Us:** 30-150 F&B-specific metrics (food cost %, menu engineering, labor productivity)

**Talking Point:**
> "Their templates are for 'any business' - retail, consulting, SaaS. Ours are built FOR F&B. We have food cost %, top-selling items, menu profitability matrix - metrics that matter to your restaurant."

---

**4. Bahasa Indonesia Content**
- **Them:** English (creator is usually US/Europe based)
- **Us:** Full Bahasa Indonesia (videos, guides, support)

**Talking Point:**
> "Their tutorials are in English. You'll spend hours Google Translating or guessing. Our videos, guides, and support are 100% Bahasa Indonesia."

---

### LOSE

**When to Concede:**

**If prospect says:** "I'm very tech-savvy, I just need the file, I'll figure it out myself"
- **Response:** "If you're confident you can set up Power BI from a .pbix file alone, the Rp 500K templates might work for you. Our value is in the hand-holding and F&B specificity. If you don't need that, we're probably overkill."
- **Why concede:** Fighting tech-savvy DIYers is a losing battle (they won't value our support)

**If prospect says:** "I only have Rp 1M budget, can't afford Rp 4.7M"
- **Response:** "Understood - budget constraints are real. You could try a Rp 500K template first. If it doesn't work out, we'll be here with the 30-day guarantee option."
- **Why concede:** Can't sell to someone with no budget (waste of sales time)

---

### TRAPS

**Misleading Claim 1:** "Our template works with any business"

**Counter:**
> "That's true, but 'works with any business' means it's not optimized for ANY specific business. You wouldn't use a Swiss Army knife when you need a chef's knife. We're the chef's knife for F&B - purpose-built, not general-purpose."

---

**Misleading Claim 2:** "You just need to customize it a bit"

**Counter:**
> "That 'bit of customization' is where 60% of people fail. You need to know Power BI DAX formulas, Power Query transformations, data modeling. That's 40+ hours of learning. Or you can use our pre-built solution that works Day 1."

## Battle Card 2: vs POS Built-In Analytics (Moka, Pawoon, Qasir)

### KNOW

**Who They Are:**
- Indonesian POS (Point of Sale) software providers
- Moka: ~30K merchants, Pawoon: ~20K, Qasir: ~10K
- Free analytics included with POS subscription (Rp 0-200K/month POS cost)

**What They Offer:**
- Basic dashboards (5-10 metrics: revenue, top items, transaction count)
- Mobile apps (check metrics on phone)
- Real-time data (synced with POS transactions)

**Market Share:** 50% of SAM (most F&B uses POS, gets free analytics)

**Target Customer:** POS users who don't need deep analytics

---

### WIN

**Our Advantages:**

**1. Metric Depth**
- **Them:** 5-10 basic metrics (revenue today, top 5 items, transaction count)
- **Us:** 30-150 F&B metrics (menu engineering, customer RFM, labor productivity, forecasting)

**Talking Point:**
> "Your POS tells you WHAT happened - revenue today is Rp 5M, top item is cappuccino. We tell you WHY and WHAT TO DO - why is food cost up 3%, which menu items should you kill, when should you hire more staff. Surface-level vs strategic."

---

**2. Multi-POS Consolidation**
- **Them:** Locked to one POS (Moka analytics only works with Moka POS)
- **Us:** Works with ANY POS (Moka, Pawoon, Qasir, manual Excel)

**Talking Point:**
> "What if you want to switch from Moka to Pawoon next year? You lose all your analytics history. We work with any POS - you're not vendor-locked."

---

**3. Multi-Outlet Intelligence**
- **Them:** Separate dashboards per outlet (can't consolidate across 5 outlets easily)
- **Us:** Single consolidated view (all outlets in one dashboard)

**Talking Point:**
> "If you have 3 outlets, you're checking 3 separate Moka dashboards. We give you one view: compare Outlet 1 vs 2 vs 3 side-by-side, see which is most profitable."

---

**4. Advanced Analysis**
- **Them:** No menu engineering, no customer segmentation, no forecasting
- **Us:** Menu profitability matrix, VIP customer tracking, 30/60/90-day revenue forecasts

**Talking Point:**
> "Moka shows you your bestsellers. We show you which bestsellers are actually PROFITABLE (high volume but low margin = bad). That's the difference."

---

### LOSE

**When to Concede:**

**If prospect says:** "I only have 1 outlet, I'm happy with free Moka analytics"
- **Response:** "If you're satisfied with what Moka gives you and don't need more depth, stick with it. We're the upgrade when you outgrow basic reports."
- **Why concede:** Can't compete with "free" for basic needs

**If prospect says:** "I don't have budget for paid analytics, I use Moka because it's free"
- **Response:** "Totally understand. One thing to consider: if Moka analytics helped you discover a Rp 2M/month cost savings (like our customers do), would the Rp 4.7M one-time investment make sense? If not, we're not a fit right now."
- **Why concede:** No budget = no sale

---

### TRAPS

**Misleading Claim 1:** "POS analytics is free, yours costs Rp 4.7M"

**Counter:**
> "It's free, but limited. Here's the real question: would you rather have 10 free metrics that give you surface-level insights, or invest Rp 4.7M to get 70 metrics that could save you Rp 2-4M/month? Our beta customers saved Rp 28.8M/year on average. That's a 6x ROI."

---

**Misleading Claim 2:** "We're adding more features to POS analytics soon"

**Counter:**
> "That's great! But 'soon' could be 6-12 months. Can your business wait that long? Our customers discovered Rp 2M/month in savings within 30 days. Every month you wait is Rp 2M left on the table."

## Battle Card 3: vs Hiring Junior Analyst

### KNOW

**What It Is:**
- Hiring a full-time or part-time junior data analyst
- Typical salary: Rp 5-8M/month (Rp 60-96M/year)
- Fresh graduate or 1-2 years experience

**What They Offer:**
- Dedicated resource (works for you 40 hours/week)
- Fully custom analysis (builds exactly what you need)
- Ad-hoc questions (can ask anything anytime)
- Human expertise (not just a tool)

**Market Share:** 10-15% of SAM (small businesses that can afford analyst)

**Target Customer:** Businesses with Rp 5B+ revenue, 5+ outlets, complex data needs

---

### WIN

**Our Advantages:**

**1. Cost (95% Cheaper)**
- **Them:** Rp 60-96M/year (Rp 5-8M/month × 12 months)
- **Us:** Rp 4.7M one-time (works forever)

**Talking Point:**
> "A junior analyst costs Rp 60M in Year 1. We cost Rp 4.7M. That's 92% cheaper. After 12 months, you've spent Rp 60M on an analyst. We're still Rp 4.7M, forever."

---

**2. Speed to Value**
- **Them:** 3-6 months ramp-up (learning your business, building dashboards, training)
- **Us:** <24 hours to first insights (dashboard ready Day 1)

**Talking Point:**
> "It takes 3-6 months to train a junior analyst. We work from Day 1. If you need insights THIS month (not in 6 months), we're the faster option."

---

**3. Zero HR Burden**
- **Them:** Recruit (2-4 weeks), train (3 months), manage (ongoing), retain (30-50% annual turnover)
- **Us:** Buy once, use forever (zero HR overhead)

**Talking Point:**
> "Analysts quit. Our beta customers had 30-50% analyst turnover/year. Every time they leave, you start over - 3 months training wasted. Our dashboard doesn't quit."

---

**4. F&B Domain Expertise**
- **Them:** Generalist (learns F&B on the job over 3-6 months)
- **Us:** F&B specialist (100 F&B metrics pre-built from Day 1)

**Talking Point:**
> "A junior analyst is a generalist - they need 6 months to learn F&B. We have 10 years of F&B expertise baked in. Food cost %, menu engineering, labor productivity - these are already built."

---

### LOSE

**When to Concede:**

**If prospect says:** "I need ad-hoc analysis, not just dashboards (e.g., 'Why did revenue drop in Outlet 3 last week?')"
- **Response:** "You're right - we're great for systematic reporting, but not ad-hoc deep-dives. If you need a human to investigate one-off questions weekly, an analyst is better. We're for the 80% recurring questions, not the 20% unique ones."
- **Why concede:** Can't replace human flexibility with templates

**If prospect says:** " I'm a 20-outlet chain with Rp 50B revenue, I need a full analyst team"
- **Response:** "At your scale, you absolutely need analysts. We're right-sized for 1-15 outlets. Above that, you need Product 06 (Custom Dashboards, Rp 25-50M) or a full analyst team."
- **Why concede:** Too big for our template solution

---

### TRAPS

**Misleading Claim 1:** "An analyst can do things your template can't"

**Counter:**
> "True - an analyst is more flexible. But ask yourself: how often do you need that flexibility? 80% of your questions are recurring: 'What's my food cost this month? Which items are bestsellers? How's Outlet 2 performing?' We answer those 80% for 1/12th the cost. Hire an analyst for the 20% if you need it."

---

**Misleading Claim 2:** "Analysts provide strategic thinking, templates don't"

**Counter:**
> "Analysts provide strategic thinking after 6-12 months of experience with your business. In Month 1-6, they're just building dashboards - exactly what our template already has. So you're paying Rp 30M for them to reinvent what we've already built, then MAYBE getting strategic value after 6 months. We give you the dashboards NOW."

## Battle Card 4: vs Enterprise BI (Tableau, Qlik, Power BI Pro)

### KNOW

**Who They Are:**
- Enterprise BI software: Tableau, Qlik Sense, Microsoft Power BI Pro/Premium
- Pricing: Rp 3-5M/month per user = Rp 36-60M/year
- Target: Large enterprises (100+ outlets, 1000+ employees)

**What They Offer:**
- Highly customizable (build anything)
- Multi-user collaboration (10-100 users share dashboards)
- Enterprise features (row-level security, governance, certified datasets)
- Cloud-hosted (access anywhere)

**Market Share:** <5% of SAM (too expensive for SMEs)

**Target Customer:** Enterprise chains (50+ outlets)

---

### WIN

**Our Advantages:**

**1. Price (66-80% Cheaper Year 1)**
- **Them:** Rp 36-60M/year (Rp 3-5M/month × 12)
- **Us:** Rp 10-12M one-time (Tier 3)

**Talking Point:**
> "Tableau costs Rp 50M/year. We're Rp 12M one-time. In Year 1, you save Rp 38M. In Year 2, we're still Rp 12M total, they're Rp 100M cumulative. Right-sized pricing for SME F&B."

---

**2. Faster Setup (30x)**
- **Them:** 3-6 months setup (need consultant, build from scratch)
- **Us:** 3-5 days white-glove setup (pre-built F&B dashboards)

**Talking Point:**
> "Tableau takes 6 months to see value. We deliver in 1 week. If you're opening a new outlet next month and need insights NOW, we're 26 weeks faster."

---

**3. No Learning Curve**
- **Them:** Steep learning (40+ hours training, complex interface)
- **Us:** Gentle (F&B-specific, pre-built, 2 hours training)

**Talking Point:**
> "Tableau has a 2-month learning curve. Your staff won't use it (too complex). We designed ours for F&B operators - if you can read a menu, you can read our dashboard."

---

**4. Right-Sized for SME**
- **Them:** Built for 100-outlet chains (overkill for 5 outlets)
- **Us:** Built for 1-15 outlets (exactly your size)

**Talking Point:**
> "Tableau is like buying a 747 when you need a Cessna. Yes, it can do more - but do you need to fly 300 passengers across continents? Or do you need to get from Jakarta to Bandung with 5 people? We're the right vehicle for your journey."

---

### LOSE

**When to Concede:**

**If prospect says:** "We're a 50-outlet chain, we need multi-user collaboration for 20 analysts"
- **Response:** "At your scale, Tableau makes sense. We're designed for 1-15 outlets with 1-3 dashboard users. You've outgrown us. Consider Product 13 (SaaS Enterprise) when it launches in 2028, or stick with Tableau."
- **Why concede:** Too big for our solution

**If prospect says:** "We need extreme customization - connect to Oracle ERP, SAP, custom data lakes"
- **Response:** "We work with Excel and basic CSV imports. If you have enterprise data infrastructure, Tableau or Product 06 (Custom Development) is better. We're pre-built templates, not infinitely customizable."
- **Why concede:** Wrong technical fit

---

### TRAPS

**Misleading Claim 1:** "Tableau can do everything, your template is limited"

**Counter:**
> "True - Tableau is more powerful. But here's the question: would you rather have 100% customization in 6 months, or 80% perfect in 1 week? Our beta customers got insights in <24 hours. How much is being blind for 6 months costing you?"

---

**Misleading Claim 2:** "Tableau is industry-standard, everyone uses it"

**Counter:**
> "Tableau is industry-standard for Fortune 500 companies with 10-analyst teams and Rp 1B budgets. You're a 5-outlet café. You don't need a Formula 1 car - you need a reliable Honda. We're the Honda: affordable, reliable, perfect for your needs."

## Battle Card 5: vs Do Nothing (Manual Excel)

### KNOW

**What It Is:**
- Continuing with manual Excel (status quo)
- Owner/staff spend 4-8 hours/week on reporting
- Cost: "Free" (sunk cost - Excel already owned)

**Market Share:** 80% of TAM (most F&B still uses manual Excel)

**Target Customer:** Everyone (this is the default state)

---

### WIN

**Why Change from Excel:**

**1. Time Savings (97%)**
- **Them:** 4 hours/week × 52 weeks = 208 hours/year wasted
- **Us:** 10 min/week × 52 weeks = 8.7 hours/year
- **Savings:** 199.3 hours/year = Rp 99.65M (at Rp 500K/hour owner rate)

**Talking Point:**
> "You're wasting 4 hours every Sunday on Excel. That's 208 hours/year - worth Rp 100M at your owner rate. We give you that time back. Use those 4 hours to open a 2nd outlet, spend time with family, or sleep."

---

**2. Error Reduction (100%)**
- **Them:** Manual copy-paste, formula errors, version control chaos
- **Us:** Automated refresh, zero manual entry, single source of truth

**Talking Point:**
> "How many times have you caught a formula error AFTER presenting to investors? Or spent 30 minutes finding 'the latest version'? We eliminate manual errors."

---

**3. Insights You're Missing**
- **Them:** Excel shows data, not insights (you still need to analyze)
- **Us:** Pre-built analyses (menu engineering, customer RFM, trend forecasting)

**Talking Point:**
> "Excel gives you data tables. We give you insights. Example: Your Excel shows cappuccino is your bestseller. Our dashboard shows cappuccino has 15% profit margin while latte has 35% margin - you should push latte more. That's the difference."

---

**4. Scalability**
- **Them:** Excel breaks at 2+ outlets (manual consolidation nightmare)
- **Us:** Built for multi-outlet from Day 1 (consolidate 15 outlets in one view)

**Talking Point:**
> "Excel worked when you had 1 outlet. Now you have 3. How much time do you spend consolidating 3 Excel files? 8 hours/week? We auto-consolidate in 10 minutes."

---

### LOSE

**When to Concede:**

**If prospect says:** "I only check my numbers once a month, I'm not data-driven"
- **Response:** "If you're not data-driven and happy with that, we're not a fit. Our customers are owners who WANT to be data-driven but lack the tools. We can't force data culture."
- **Why concede:** Can't sell to someone who doesn't value data

**If prospect says:** "Excel works fine for me, I've used it for 10 years"
- **Response:** "If Excel truly works fine, keep using it. We're here when you hit Excel's limits - usually around 2-3 outlets or Rp 5B revenue. We'll be here when you're ready."
- **Why concede:** Change is hard; don't force it

---

### TRAPS

**Misleading Claim 1:** "Excel is free, yours costs Rp 4.7M"

**Counter:**
> "Excel is free, but your TIME isn't. You spend 4 hours/week on Excel = Rp 8M/month in opportunity cost (your owner rate). That's Rp 96M/year wasted on Excel. We cost Rp 4.7M and save you Rp 96M/year. Which is really 'free'?"

---

**Misleading Claim 2:** "I'm comfortable with Excel, I don't want to learn new tools"

**Counter:**
> "You don't need to learn Power BI. You keep using Excel - just fill our templates (same Excel you already know). Then click ONE button in Power BI to see your dashboard. That's it. If you can click 'Save' in Excel, you can use our tool."
