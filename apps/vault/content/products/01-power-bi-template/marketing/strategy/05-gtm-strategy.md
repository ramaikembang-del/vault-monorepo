## Part 3: Growth Phase (Weeks 6-16, Feb 12 - June 4)

### Channel Strategy

**Channel 1: Facebook Groups (60% of leads)**

**Tactics:**
- Join 20-25 active F&B groups (5K-10K members each)
- Post 2x/week (Monday, Thursday)
- Content mix:
 - 50% educational (free tips, no product pitch)
 - 30% case studies (customer success stories)
 - 20% direct promotion (with value proposition)

**Post Schedule:**

**Week 6-8 (Educational Focus):**
- "5 Excel mistakes F&B owners make (and how to fix them)"
- "How to calculate food cost % correctly"
- "Free Excel calculator: Is your food cost too high?"

**Week 9-11 (Case Study Focus):**
- "How Dimas saved Rp 2M/month with BI analytics"
- "From 4 hours/week Excel to 10 minutes (customer story)"
- "Multi-outlet chaos to clarity (3-outlet café case study)"

**Week 12-16 (Direct Promotion):**
- "Power BI for F&B: 100 customers, 92% setup success"
- "Special offer: First 100 customers get 10% off"
- "30-day money-back guarantee - try risk-free"

**Expected Performance:**
- 250K group reach (25 groups × 10K members avg)
- 2,500 post views (1% engagement)
- 125 clicks (5% CTR)
- 60 leads (48% lead conversion)
- 36 sales (60% lead -> sale conversion)

**Cost:** Rp 0 (organic reach)

---

**Channel 2: POS Partnerships (30% of leads)**

**Target Partners:**
1. Moka (30K merchants)
2. Pawoon (20K merchants)
3. Qasir (10K merchants)

**Partnership Structure:**
- We: Provide 10-20% revenue share on each sale
- They: Email blast to customers (1x/month) + app notification

**Negotiation Timeline:**
- Week 6-8: Initial outreach, pitch deck
- Week 9-10: Pilot with 1 POS (Qasir - smallest, easiest)
- Week 11-12: Negotiate contracts (Moka, Pawoon)
- Week 13-16: Execute campaigns

**Expected Performance:**
- Qasir blast: 10K reach -> 200 clicks -> 30 leads -> 18 sales
- Moka blast: 30K reach -> 600 clicks -> 90 leads -> 54 sales (pending contract)

**Cost:** Rp 30M (10% revenue share on 72 sales @ Rp 4.7M = Rp 34M revenue × 10% = Rp 3.4M, but negotiated upfront as Rp 30M pilot budget)

---

**Channel 3: Referrals (10% of leads)**

**Referral Program:**
- Referrer: Rp 1M cash reward
- Referee: Rp 500K discount

**Activation:**
- Email all customers (weekly): "Know anyone struggling with Excel? Get Rp 1M"
- WhatsApp blast (monthly): Same message
- In-product prompt: "Refer a friend" button in dashboard

**Expected Performance:**
- 100 customers × 20% referral rate = 20 referrals
- 20 referrals × 50% conversion = 10 sales

**Cost:** Rp 15M (10 sales × Rp 1.5M avg referral cost)

## Part 5: Sales Execution

### Sales Team Structure (Sprint 1)

**Founder/CEO (Sales Lead):**
- Focus: Tier 2-3 customers (high-touch)
- Capacity: 5-10 deals/week
- Close rate: 70% (experienced seller)

**Sales Rep 1 (Hire Week 8):**
- Focus: Tier 1 customers (low-touch)
- Capacity: 10-15 deals/week
- Close rate: 50% (junior)

**Total Capacity:**
- Weeks 5-7: 15-30 leads/week (founder only)
- Weeks 8-16: 30-50 leads/week (founder + rep)

---

### Sales Process by Tier

**Tier 1 (70% of sales):**
1. Lead inquiry (Facebook DM, email)
2. Respond with one-pager + pricing (same day)
3. Answer questions via email (1-2 exchanges)
4. Send payment link
5. Close (avg 3-7 days from inquiry to close)

**Tier 2 (25% of sales):**
1. Lead inquiry
2. Schedule discovery call (30 min, within 48 hours)
3. Custom demo (30 min, 2-3 days later)
4. Send proposal (same day as demo)
5. Follow-up (2-3 days later)
6. Close (avg 14-21 days)

**Tier 3 (5% of sales):**
1. Lead inquiry
2. Discovery call (60 min, within 24 hours)
3. Executive demo (30 min, 3-5 days later)
4. POC/Trial (14 days)
5. Proposal + negotiation (7-14 days)
6. Close (avg 30-45 days)

---

### CRM Workflow

**Lead Stages:**
1. **New Lead** (unqualified)
2. **Qualified** (budget + fit confirmed)
3. **Demo Scheduled**
4. **Proposal Sent**
5. **Negotiation**
6. **Closed-Won** or **Closed-Lost**

**Automation:**
- Stage 1 -> 2: Auto-email (qualification questions)
- Stage 3: Calendar invite sent
- Stage 4: Proposal template auto-populated
- Closed-Won: Auto-trigger CS onboarding email

## Part 8: Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Low Lead Volume

**Scenario:** Only 50 leads/week instead of 100

**Impact:** Miss revenue target by 50% (Rp 250M vs Rp 508M)

**Mitigation:**
- Activate paid ads (Facebook, Google) - Rp 25M budget
- Accelerate POS partnerships (launch Week 6 instead of Week 13)
- Double referral incentive (Rp 2M referrer, Rp 1M referee)

---

### Risk 2: Low Conversion Rate

**Scenario:** 30% lead -> sale instead of 60%

**Impact:** Miss revenue target by 50%

**Mitigation:**
- Improve sales playbook (more objection handling)
- Offer discounts (10-15% early-bird)
- Add 60-day money-back guarantee (vs 30-day)

---

### Risk 3: High Churn (Setup Failures)

**Scenario:** 50% setup failure rate (vs 92% target)

**Impact:** 50% refunds, negative word-of-mouth

**Mitigation:**
- White-glove setup for ALL customers (not just Tier 3)
- Hire additional CS rep (Week 6 instead of Week 9)
- Simplify Excel templates (reduce data entry fields)
