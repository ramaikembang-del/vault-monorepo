## Week 2: Contribute

### Write Your First PRD
**Practice:** Pick a small feature request, write full PRD

**PRD Template:**
```markdown
# [Feature Name]

**Problem:** What customer pain are we solving?
**User Story:** As a [user], I want [goal], so that [benefit]
**Success Metrics:** How do we measure success?
**User Flow:** Step-by-step how users will use this
**Requirements:**
- Functional requirements (what it does)
- Non-functional (performance, security, etc.)
**Designs:** Link to mockups
**Technical Notes:** For engineering team
**Launch Plan:** Beta → Full rollout
**Risks:** What could go wrong?
```

### Work with Engineering
- [ ] Present PRD to engineering team
- [ ] Incorporate feedback
- [ ] Break down into user stories
- [ ] Prioritize backlog with tech lead

## Month 1-3: Ramp Up

### Month 1: Ship Small Feature
- Own 1 small feature end-to-end

- Get comfortable saying "no" to low-priority requests

### Month 3: Strategic Thinking

- Present to leadership with business case
- Drive OKR for your product area
- Mentor junior PM (if hired)

## Key Skills

### Customer Research
**Talk to customers constantly:**
- Weekly customer interviews (5-10 per month minimum)
- Join support tickets reviews
- Analyze usage data (Mixpanel)
- Run surveys, NPS tracking

**Best Practices:**
- Ask "why" 5 times (get to root cause)
- Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule)
- Look for patterns across customers
- Validate with data, not just opinions

### Prioritization
**Framework: RICE**
- **Reach:** How many users affected?
- **Impact:** How much value per user?
- **Confidence:** How sure are we?
- **Effort:** How long to build?

**Score = (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort**

### Stakeholder Management
- **Engineering:** Be clear on requirements, respect their expertise
- **Sales:** Understand what's blocking deals
- **CS:** They know customer pain best
- **Leadership:** Show business impact (revenue, retention, efficiency)

## Success Metrics (90 Days)

**Month 1:**
- Ship 1 small feature
- Write 3 PRDs (1 shipped, 2 in backlog)
- Talk to 10+ customers

**Month 2:**
- Own 1 product area
- Define OKRs for your product

**Month 3:**
- Drive measurable business impact (revenue, retention, efficiency)
- Present product strategy to leadership
- Become go-to expert for your product area

## Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge: Engineering says "too hard to build"**
- Solution: Ask "what would make it easier?" Find MVP version.

**Challenge: Too many feature requests**
- Solution: Prioritize ruthlessly. Say no to 90% of requests.

**Challenge: Customers want everything**
- Solution: Dig deeper into "why". Often there's a simpler solution.

**Challenge: Sales wants feature X "to close deal"**
- Solution: Is it one customer or pattern? Validate with data.
