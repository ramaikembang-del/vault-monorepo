## Sample Exercise

# Exercise: Create Your First Sales Dashboard

**Objective:** Learn to create a basic sales dashboard showing daily sales trends
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Prerequisites:** E1 Dashboard Lite account with test data

---

## Scenario

You're the owner of "Cafe Bandung", and you want to see your sales trends for the last 7 days to identify your busiest days.

---

## Materials Needed

- [ ] E1 Dashboard Lite account (test environment)
- [ ] Test data loaded (last 30 days of cafe sales)

---

## Instructions

### Step 1: Log in to Dashboard
1. Go to app.pwbi.id
2. Log in with test credentials
3. You should see the home dashboard

**Expected outcome:** Dashboard loads showing overview widgets

### Step 2: Create New Dashboard
1. Click "+ New Dashboard" in top right
2. Name it "Sales Trends - Last 7 Days"
3. Click "Create"

### Step 3: Add Sales Chart Widget
1. Click "+ Add Widget"
2. Select "Line Chart"
3. Configure:
 - Metric: Total Sales
 - Group by: Day
 - Date range: Last 7 days
4. Click "Add to Dashboard"

**Expected outcome:** Line chart showing daily sales for last week

### Step 4: Analyze the Data
Look at your chart and answer:
- Which day had highest sales?
- Which day had lowest sales?
- Is there a pattern (e.g., weekends vs weekdays)?

---

## Success Criteria

- [ ] Dashboard created and named correctly
- [ ] Chart shows 7 days of data
- [ ] Can identify highest and lowest sales days

---

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake:** Chart shows wrong date range
**Solution:** Click chart settings â†’ Change date range to "Last 7 days"

**Mistake:** Chart is empty
**Solution:** Check that test data is loaded. Refresh page.

---

## Answer Key

**Expected chart:** Line graph with 7 data points
**Highest sales day:** Should be Saturday (test data pattern)
**Lowest sales day:** Should be Monday

---

## Debrief Questions

1. What insights did you get from this dashboard?
2. How would you use this in your real restaurant?
3. What other metrics would be useful to add?

---

## Variations

**Easier:** Pre-create dashboard, just have them change date range
**Harder:** Add comparison to previous week (14-day view)

---

**Estimated completion time:** 20-30 minutes
