# Code of Conduct

**Effective Date:** December 2025
**Scope:** All team members, contractors, partners
**Owner:** HR + Leadership

---

## Our Commitment

PWBI is committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and inclusive environment for everyone, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, disability, ethnicity, religion, or age.

**This Code of Conduct applies to:**
- Office/workplace
- Remote work
- Company events
- Online communication (Slack, email, social media)
- Customer interactions
- Partner/vendor interactions

---

## Expected Behavior

### Be Respectful
- Treat everyone with dignity
- Listen actively, speak thoughtfully
- Value diverse perspectives
- Disagree professionally (ideas, not people)

### Be Professional
- Deliver quality work
- Meet commitments
- Communicate proactively
- Own your mistakes

### Be Collaborative
- Help teammates succeed
- Share knowledge freely
- Credit others' contributions
- Assume positive intent

### Be Inclusive
- Use inclusive language
- Make space for all voices
- Challenge biases (including your own)
- Accommodate different needs

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

**Zero tolerance** for:

### Harassment
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Offensive comments (gender, race, religion, orientation, disability)
- Intimidation or threats
- Stalking (online or physical)
- Inappropriate physical contact
- Unwanted photography or recording

### Discrimination
- Bias in hiring, promotion, compensation
- Exclusion based on protected characteristics
- Microaggpressions
- Retaliation against those who report issues

### Misconduct
- Dishonesty or fraud
- Theft or misuse of company resources
- Breach of confidentiality
- Conflicts of interest (undisclosed)
- Violence or threats of violence

### Disruptive Behavior
- Sustained disruption of meetings/work
- Personal attacks
- Trolling or inflammatory comments
- Deliberate misgendering or using wrong pronouns

---

## Reporting

### How to Report

**If you experience or witness a violation:**

1. **Immediate safety concern:** Contact local authorities
2. **HR:** hr@pwbi.co.id (confidential)
3. **Leadership:** Any member of leadership team
4. **Anonymous hotline:** [Number/link]

**What to include:**
- What happened (who, what, when, where)
- Any witnesses
- Any evidence (emails, messages, etc.)
- Impact on you

### What Happens Next

1. **Acknowledgment:** Within 24 hours
2. **Investigation:** Confidential, impartial (1-2 weeks typically)
3. **Action:** Appropriate response based on findings
4. **Follow-up:** Check-in with reporter

### Confidentiality

- Reports handled with discretion
- Shared only on need-to-know basis
- Reporter's identity protected (except where legally required)

### No Retaliation

**Retaliation is prohibited.**

Reporting in good faith will **never** result in negative consequences, even if the report is ultimately unsubstantiated.

Retaliation itself is a violation and will result in disciplinary action.

---

## Consequences

Violations will result in consequences proportional to severity:

**Minor (first-time, unintentional):**
- Private conversation
- Coaching
- Apology to affected party

**Moderate (pattern, or significant impact):**
- Written warning
- Mandatory training
- Temporary suspension
- Reassignment

**Severe (egregious, or repeated):**
- Termination
- Legal action (if applicable)
- Reporting to authorities (if criminal)

**Leadership held to higher standard** - violations may result in immediate termination.

## Specific Scenarios

### Communication Guidelines

**Email/Slack:**
- Professional tone
- No ALL CAPS (yelling)
- No passive-aggressive comments
- Assume good intent (ask for clarification)

**Meetings:**
- One speaker at a time
- Don't interrupt
- Avoid sidebar conversations
- Video on for team meetings (camera ok to be off for personal reasons)

**Feedback:**
- Focus on behavior/work, not person
- Specific examples
- Deliver privately (not in group chat)
- Timely (don't let issues fester)

### Social Events

**Even at social events, this Code applies:**
- Moderate alcohol consumption (if drinking)
- No pressure to drink
- Respect boundaries
- Safe transportation (we provide)

### Remote Work

**Respect boundaries:**
- Cameras off is ok (don't demand)
- Home backgrounds are private (don't comment)
- Flexible hours (within reason)
- After-hours messages ok, responses not expected

## Diversity &Inclusion

### Our Commitment
- Diverse team reflects diverse customers
- Inclusion is everyone's responsibility
- Equity in opportunity (hiring, promotion, compensation)
- Regular bias training

### In Practice
- Interview panels are diverse
- Blind resume screening (where possible)
- Pronoun sharing encouraged (not mandatory)
- Religious/cultural accommodations provided
- Accessibility accommodations provided

---

## Conflicts of Interest

**Must disclose:**
- Outside employment
- Financial interests in competitors/vendors
- Hiring/managing family members
- Relationships with coworkers (if in reporting line)

**How to disclose:** Email hr@pwbi.co.id, reviewed case-by-case

---

## Confidentiality

**Protect:**
- Customer data (GDPR, privacy laws)
- Company financials
- Product roadmap (until public)
- Employee information
- Trade secrets

**Don't:**
- Share customer lists with competitors
- Post internal metrics on social media
- Discuss salaries publicly (ok to discuss with coworkers)
- Take confidential info when leaving company

---

## Social Media

**You can:**
- Share your PWBI experience
- Express opinions (clearly personal, not company)
- Post job openings

**Don't:**
- Speak on behalf of company (unless authorized)
- Share confidential information
- Harass customers/competitors
- violate other aspects of this Code

**Guideline:** "Would I be comfortable if this was on front page of newspaper?"
