## Overview

**Purpose:** Systematically reduce customer churn through proactive intervention, health monitoring, and targeted retention campaigns.

**Success Metrics:**
- Monthly churn rate: <10%
- Annual renewal rate: >80%
- Win-back rate: >15%
- NPS from at-risk customers: >30

## Churn Categories & Root Causes

### Category 1: Product Issues (30% of churn)

**Symptoms:**
- "Dashboard too slow"
- "Missing features I need"
- "Too complicated to use"

**Solutions:**
- Performance optimization guide
- Feature request prioritization
- 1-on-1 training session
- Consider product improvements

---

### Category 2: Price Sensitivity (25% of churn)

**Symptoms:**
- "Too expensive for my business"
- "Can't justify the cost"
- "Found cheaper alternative"

**Solutions:**
- Downgrade to lower tier (retain some revenue)
- Annual prepay discount (lock in for longer)
- ROI demonstration (show value delivered)
- Custom pricing for multi-outlet

---

### Category 3: Business Closure (20% of churn)

**Symptoms:**
- Restaurant closed
- Business pivoted away from F&B
- Owner sold business

**Solutions:**
- Graceful exit (no retention attempt)
- Exit survey (learn why business failed)
- Stay in touch (may return or refer)

---

### Category 4: Competitive Loss (15% of churn)

**Symptoms:**
- Switched to competitor
- POS vendor offers free analytics
- Consultant recommended different tool

**Solutions:**
- Competitive battle card (why we're better)
- Match competitor pricing (selectively)
- Highlight unique features (data moats, integrations)
- Win-back campaign after 3 months

---

### Category 5: Poor Onboarding (10% of churn)

**Symptoms:**
- Never completed setup
- Didn't understand value
- Too busy to learn

**Solutions:**
- Revamp onboarding (simplify)
- Mandatory kickoff call (don't skip)
- Quick win checklist (get value fast)

## Churn Postmortem Process

**After Every Churn:**

1. **Exit Survey** (send to canceling customer):
   - Why are you leaving?
   - What could we have done better?
   - Would you recommend us to others?
   - Can we stay in touch?

2. **Internal Review:**
   - What was customer health score?
   - Did we miss early warning signals?
   - Was intervention attempted? When?
   - Root cause category?

3. **Action Items:**
   - Product improvement needed?
   - Sales/onboarding gap to fix?
   - Competitive threat to address?
   - Process improvement?

4. **Document in Churn Log:**
   - Customer name, product, MRR lost
   - Churn reason (category)
   - Preventable? Y/N
   - Lessons learned

## Monthly Churn Review Meeting

**Agenda:**
1. Review churn rate (Month-over-Month, Year-over-Year)
2. Analyze churn by category (product, price, competition, etc.)
3. Review health score distribution (how many at-risk?)
4. Win-back campaign results
5. Process improvements
6. Action items for next month

**Metrics to Track:**
- Monthly churn rate (%)
- MRR churned
- Churn by product tier (Lite, Basic, Pro)
- Churn by cohort (month joined)
- Average customer lifespan
- Churn reasons distribution
