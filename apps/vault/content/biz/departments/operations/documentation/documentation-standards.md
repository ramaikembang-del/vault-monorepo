# Documentation Standards
**Writing & Maintaining Quality Documentation**

**Owner:** Operations Team  
**Last Updated:** January 8, 2026  
**Status:** ACTIVE

---

## Documentation Philosophy

**Good documentation:**
- ✅ Answers user questions WITHOUT requiring support ticket
- ✅ Uses simple language (Bahasa Indonesia for customers, English for internal)
- ✅ Includes examples and screenshots
- ❌ Never outdated (update with every product change)

---

## Document Types

### 1. Customer-Facing Docs
- **Help Center:** How-to guides, FAQs
- **Product Docs:** Feature explanations
- **Tutorials:** Step-by-step walkthroughs (video + text)

**Standard:** Bahasa Indonesia, screenshots, <500 words per article

### 2. Internal Docs
- **Runbooks:** Operational procedures (SOPs)
- **Strategy Docs:** Planning, frameworks
- **Technical Docs:** Architecture, APIs

**Standard:** English OK, detailed, cross-linked

---

## Writing Standards

### Structure (Every Document)
1. **Title:** Clear, action-oriented ("How to Upload CSV Data")
2. **Summary:** 1-2 sentences (what this doc covers)
3. **Body:** Numbered steps or logical sections
4. **Related Links:** 2-3 relevant documents

### Language Guidelines
- **Active voice:** "Click Upload" (not "Upload should be clicked")
- **Short sentences:** <20 words per sentence
- **Bullet points:** For lists, not long paragraphs
- **Examples:** Always include 1-2 real examples

---

## Maintenance Schedule

**Quarterly Review:**
- Check all customer-facing docs for accuracy
- Update screenshots if UI changed
- Archive outdated content

**Every Product Release:**
- Update affected docs within 48 hours
- Notify CS team of changes

---

## Tools

**Documentation Platform:** Notion (internal + customer help center)  
**Screenshot Tool:** CleanShot or Snagit  
**Video:** Loom (max 5 min per video)
