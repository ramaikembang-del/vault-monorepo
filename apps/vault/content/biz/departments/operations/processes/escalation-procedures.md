# Escalation Procedures
**Managing Critical Issues & Customer Escalations**

**Owner:** Operations Team  
**Status:** ACTIVE

---

## Escalation Levels

### Level 1: Frontline Support (CS Specialist)
- **Scope:** Standard tickets, how-to questions
- **Response:** 24h
- **Escalate if:** Unable to resolve within 48h OR customer demands manager

### Level 2: CS Manager
- **Scope:** Complex issues, billing disputes, feature complaints
- **Response:** 8h
- **Escalate if:** Requires product changes OR customer threatens churn

### Level 3: Product Team
- **Scope:** Bugs, technical issues, feature requests
- **Response:** 24h
- **Escalate if:** System-wide outage OR data loss

###Level 4: Founder
- **Scope:** Enterprise escalations, legal threats, PR risks
- **Response:** ASAP
- **Trigger:** Customer explicitly asks for founder OR revenue >Rp 2M/month at risk

---

## Escalation Criteria

**CRITICAL (Level 3-4):**
- System downtime >1 hour
- Data loss or security breach
- Customer threatens lawsuit
- NPS <3 from enterprise customer

**HIGH (Level 2):**
- Customer demands refund
- Multiple support tickets (3+ in 7 days)
- Negative public review

**MEDIUM (Level 1):**
- Feature not working as expected
- Billing confusion
- Onboarding struggling

---

## Escalation Process

1. **Log Issue:** CRM ticket with priority tag
2. **Notify Manager:** Slack message + email
3. **Acknowledge Customer:** "Escalated to senior team, will respond within [timeframe]"
4. **Resolve:** Manager/Product/Founder takes over
5. **Post-Mortem:** Document learnings (for recurring issues)

---

## Customer Communication

**During Escalation:**
- Set clear expectations ("You'll hear from us by 5pm today")
- Provide updates every 4-8h (don't go dark)
- Apologize sincerely (acknowledge frustration)

**After Resolution:**
- Follow-up call/email (ensure satisfied)
- Offer goodwill gesture if appropriate (credit, discount)
- Request feedback ("How can we prevent this?")
