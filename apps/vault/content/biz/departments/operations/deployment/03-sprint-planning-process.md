# Sprint Planning Process

**Last Updated:** 2025-12-27
**Owner:** Product & Engineering

---

## Overview

2-week sprint planning process for PWBI FnB Growth engineering and product teams.

---

## Sprint Cadence

**Sprint Length:** 2 weeks (10 business days)
**Team Capacity:** Assume 6-7 productive days per person (buffer for meetings meetings, support)

---

## Sprint Ceremonies

### 1. Sprint Planning (Monday Week 1, 2 hours)

**Attendees:** Product + Engineering
**Agenda:**
1. Review sprint goal
2. Present prioritized backlog
3. Engineering estimates work (story points or hours)
4. Commit to sprint backlog
5. Break down tasks

**Output:** Sprint backlog with clear commitments

---

### 2. Daily Standup (Every day, 15 min)

See [[biz/playbooks/team-collaboration/02-meeting-rhythms|Meeting Rhythms]]

---

### 3. Sprint Review (Friday Week 2, 1 hour)

**Attendees:** Product + Engineering + Stakeholders
**Agenda:**
1. Demo completed work
2. Review sprint goal achievement
3. Get feedback from stakeholders

---

### 4. Sprint Retrospective (Friday Week 2, 45 min)

**Attendees:** Product + Engineering
**Format:**
- What went well?
- What didn't go well?
- What will we change next sprint?

**Output:** 1-3 action items for next sprint

---

## Backlog Prioritization

**Priority Framework:**
1. **P0 - Critical:** Production bugs, blockers
2. **P1 - High:** Customer commitments, roadmap features
3. **P2 - Medium:** Nice-to-haves, optimizations
4. **P3 - Low:** Future ideas, experiments

**Product Manager** maintains prioritized backlog

---

## Definition of Done

Feature is "Done" when:
- Code written and reviewed
- Tests pass (unit + integration)
- Deployed to staging
- QA testing complete
- Documentation updated
- Product manager accepts
