## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

**"Confidential Information"** means any information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, or in any other form, that:

1. **Is marked** "Confidential," "Proprietary," or similar designation; OR
2. **Should reasonably be understood** to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure

**Examples Include:**
- Business plans, strategies, financial data
- Customer lists, pricing information
- Technical data, source code, algorithms
- Trade secrets, proprietary processes
- Product roadmaps, unreleased features

---

## 2. Exclusions from Confidential Information

Confidential Information **does NOT include** information that:

1. **Was public** before disclosure or becomes public through no fault of Receiving Party
2. **Was already known** by Receiving Party before disclosure (with proof)
3. **Was independently developed** by Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information
4. **Was rightfully received** from a third party without confidentiality obligation
5. **Is required to be disclosed** by law or court order (with notice to Disclosing Party)

---

## 3. Obligations of Receiving Party

The Receiving Party agrees to:

### 3.1 Maintain Confidentiality
- **Protect** Confidential Information with same degree of care used for own confidential info (minimum: reasonable care)
- **Not disclose** Confidential Information to third parties without written consent

### 3.2 Limited Use
- Use Confidential Information **ONLY** for the purpose of [State Purpose, e.g., "evaluating potential partnership"]
- **Not use** for any other commercial purpose without consent

### 3.3 Limited Disclosure
- Disclose Confidential Information only to employees, contractors, or advisors who:
 - Have a **need to know**
 - Are bound by confidentiality obligations **at least as restrictive** as this Agreement

### 3.4 Return or Destroy
- Upon request or termination of discussions:
 - **Return or destroy** all Confidential Information (including copies)
 - **Certify in writing** that all materials have been returned/destroyed

---

## 4. Permitted Disclosures

Receiving Party **MAY** disclose Confidential Information if:

1. **Required by law** or court order
 - Must provide **advance notice** to Disclosing Party (if legally permissible)
 - Disclose only the **minimum required** to comply

2. **With written consent** from Disclosing Party

---

## 5. No Obligation to Disclose

- Neither Party is **obligated** to disclose any Confidential Information
- Each Party may **refuse** to disclose or cease disclosures at any time

---

## 6. No License or Ownership Transfer

- This Agreement does **NOT grant** any license, ownership, or rights to Confidential Information
- All Confidential Information remains the **property** of the Disclosing Party
- No implied licenses or rights beyond those explicitly stated
