# POS Partnerships Strategy

**Last Updated:** 2025-12-27
**Owner:** Partnerships Team

---

## Overview

Partnership strategy for integrating with Point-of-Sale (POS) systems, critical for embedded analytics (A3) and data sync for all products.

---

## Strategic Importance

**Why POS Partnerships Matter:**
1. **Data Source:** POS systems hold transaction data we need
2. **Distribution:** POS vendors can refer customers to us
3. **Embedded Analytics:** Partnership enables white-label embedding (A3 product)
4. **Competitive Moat:** Integration partnerships create switching costs

## Partnership Models

### Model 1: Integration Partnership (Current)

**What:**
- PWBI builds integration to POS API
- Customers connect their POS to PWBI
- Data syncs automatically

**Value to POS:**
- Value-add for their customers
- Increases stickiness (customers less likely to switch)
- Minimal effort (we build integration)

**Value to PWBI:**
- Access to customer transaction data
- Easier customer onboarding
- Better product experience

**Terms:**
- Free integration (mutual benefit)
- Co-marketing (case studies, blog posts)
- Listed in each other's partner directory

---

### Model 2: Reseller Partnership (Future Sprint 4+)

**What:**
- POS vendor sells PWBI products to their customers
- POS gets commission on sales

**Value to POS:**
- Additional revenue stream (20-30% commission)
- Upsell to existing customers

**Value to PWBI:**
- Access to POS customer base (distribution)
- Warm leads (referred by trusted POS vendor)

**Terms:**
- Commission: 20-30% of first year revenue
- POS trains sales team on PWBI features
- Co-branded marketing materials

---

### Model 3: Embedded Analytics / White-Label (A3 Product)

**What:**
- PWBI analytics embedded directly in POS dashboard
- Branded as POS feature (white-labeled)
- POS customers see analytics without leaving POS

**Value to POS:**
- Differentiation (best-in-class analytics)
- Increases perceived value of POS product
- Potential to charge premium for "Advanced Analytics" tier

**Value to PWBI:**
- Massive distribution (access to all POS customers)
- Recurring revenue share
- Brand building (though white-labeled initially)

**Terms:**
- Revenue share: 50/50 split
- PWBI builds and maintains analytics engine
- POS handles customer support (tier 1)
- Co-development roadmap

## Partnership Outreach Process

### Stage 1: Research & Warm Intro

- Research POS vendor (customer base, tech stack, strategy)
- Identify potential mutual value
- Get warm intro via investor, customer, or advisor

### Stage 2: Initial Meeting

**Agenda:**
1. Introduce PWBI (vision, traction)
2. Understand POS partner goals/challenges
3. Propose partnership model(s)
4. Gauge interest

**Output:** Proceed to pilot or not

### Stage 3: Pilot Partnership

**Scope:**
- Integrate with POS API (technical)
- 5-10 joint customers test integration
- Measure: data sync reliability, customer satisfaction
- Timeline: 1-2 months

**Success Criteria:**
- >95% data sync reliability
- >80% customer satisfaction
- <5 critical bugs

### Stage 4: Formal Partnership Agreement

**Contract Terms:**
- Partnership model (integration, reseller, embedded)
- Commission/revenue share (if applicable)
- Co-marketing commitments
- SLA/support responsibilities
- Term (12 months, auto-renew)

### Stage 5: Launch & Scale

- Announce partnership (press release, social media)
- Co-marketing (joint webinars, case studies)
- Regular business reviews (quarterly)
